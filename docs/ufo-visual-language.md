# UFO iconography as generative design reference, 1896-2026

Across 130 years of sightings and cinema, UFO imagery has congealed into **roughly a dozen reusable geometric archetypes**, each with a distinct material vocabulary, light behavior, and motion signature. This reference treats the subject as pure visual language — shapes, palettes, and procedural parameters — agnostic to belief. The arc runs from pulp-era chrome saucers to modern FLIR-grey minimalism, and the modern vocabulary is quietly the most parametric of all: a featureless primitive framed by sensor chrome, moving in physically wrong ways. That shift — from *additive* 1950s kitsch (more fins, more lights) to *subtractive* modern UAP (no wings, no exhaust, no detail) — is the single most important design insight for a generative UFO system. Everything downstream scales from that axis, including palette, primitive choice, animation vocabulary, and camera grain profile.

The report is organized for direct use in a creative-coding pipeline: each archetype ends with primitive choices, proportions, and hex palettes a shader artist can plug in.

## The era axis from chrome saucer to sensor ghost

Treat era as a master slider. The classic era (1947–1979) is defined by **additive detail, photographic authenticity tropes, and luminescence as the object**. Pulp and 1950s Hollywood codified the flat-bottom/domed-top saucer with riveted plates, portholes, tripod landing gear, and solid primary-color death-rays. *The Day the Earth Stood Still* (1951) introduced Klaatu's seamless chrome disc, establishing minimalism as an envoy trope. *Forbidden Planet* (1956) gave the C-57D its rotating central disc and Krell hieroglyphic paneling. Ray Harryhausen's *Earth vs. the Flying Saucers* (1956) locked counter-rotating rim segments around a static dome into the invasion canon. Adamski's 1952 contactee photos and Trent's 1950 McMinnville shots fed the same bell-saucer + underside-spheres grammar back into "real" photography at dusk, grainy, with overhead power-lines for scale.

The **Trumbull era (1977–1983)** inverted everything by replacing hulls with light. Spielberg's *Close Encounters* mothership, drawn from a memory of a night oil refinery, was a 300–450 meter inverted city of thousands of neon pin-lights filmed in smoke-filled rooms on 65mm — inaugurating the luminescent-no-hardware paradigm and the spectral-rainbow "conversation of lights." E.T.'s organic vine-tendril sphere with warm amber underbelly softened the palette. *V* (1983) scaled the saucer to 3–5 mile motherships hovering over world capitals.

The **grainy realist 1990s** (X-Files, *Fire in the Sky*, *Independence Day*) introduced rain-slick greys, flashlight cones cutting fog, and 15-mile concave City Destroyers with blue-white vaporization beams. The **2010s minimalist turn** — *Arrival*'s 450-meter vertical basalt Shells hovering 28 feet above ground, Turrell-influenced luminous white interiors — replaced the disc with the monolith. *Nope* (2022) biologized the classic saucer silhouette into Jean Jacket's unfolding jellyfish-angel with a single square camera-eye. *3 Body Problem* (2024) made the UFO pure information: retinal orange wavy-line countdowns, Cherenkov-blue tank glow, a mirror-perfect elongated teardrop droplet probe.

Finally, the **sensor era (2017–2026)**, opened by the December 2017 NYT article on AATIP and the April 2020 Pentagon release of FLIR1, GIMBAL, and GO FAST, is the dominant contemporary register. After the June 2021 ODNI Preliminary Assessment, the July 26 2023 House Oversight hearings with Fravor, Graves, and Grusch, and AARO's ongoing reporting, the object itself became almost incidental — **the aesthetic is now sensor-derived**: low-resolution greyscale IR, ATFLIR crosshairs, telemetry strings, WHT-HOT/BLK-HOT toggles, and featureless white primitives that defy physics.

## Classic-era archetypes, seven shapes that built the canon

**The domed saucer** (Adamski 1952, Trent 1950, *Earth vs. Flying Saucers*) is an oblate spheroid (lens) with a capsule dome on top, **aspect ratio roughly 3:1 diameter-to-height**, a rim indexed into 24–36 segments of counter-rotating lights, and — for the Adamski variant — three hemispherical "landing sphere" protrusions on the underside plus one central larger sphere. Surface is polished aluminum microfacet (metallic 1.0, roughness 0.02–0.08), primitive palette `#C8CCD0` brushed aluminum, `#E6E8EA` polished silver, rim lights `#F6E27A`, sky backdrop `#1F3A6B` cobalt.

**The mothership radial disc** (*Close Encounters* 1977, ID4 City Destroyer 1996, *V* 1983) is a high-poly radial plate (32–64 segments) with a fractal greeble layer and 1,500–5,000 punched point-lights. The ID4 variant is concave with petal-relief bas-relief undersides and a central energy cannon. CE3K's is convex with a control tower wafer inset. **Luminescence dominates hull** — the trick is bloom overriding silhouette. Palette: `#FFF3D4` neon warm white, `#FFB347` sodium amber, `#7EC8E3` mercury cyan, `#2A2D33` smoke grey backdrop.

**The pulp ray-beam saucer** uses solid-edged white/yellow cones with instant on/off, monument-crashing kinematics, Harryhausen stop-motion judder. The **abduction beam** (CE3K, *Fire in the Sky*, *Communion*) is a vertical blue-white column with slow chromatic shimmer, bottom-edge haze, and a levitating silhouette. Modern UAP inverts this entirely — the absence of a beam is the beam motif.

**The organic scout** (E.T. 1982, CE3K scouts) is a convex sphere clad in vine/tendril mesh with warm amber underbelly emission, moving slowly through forest godrays with cloud-tank smoke for volume. **Benign palette**: `#FFA62B` amber, `#B08A5B` devil's-tower ochre, `#3E2A14` dusk brown.

**The dirty-industrial hanging mothership** (*District 9* 2009) introduces rivets, scorch, rust, and long-term occlusion — a stalled UFO as infrastructure. A riveted triplanar-projected steel shader at roughness 0.6 with ambient-occlusion grit.

**The monolith shell** (*Arrival* 2016) is an ovoid/lozenge SDF at 1:2.8 aspect ratio with asteroid-noise-displaced basalt-photogrammetry surface, roughness 0.6, subtle iridescent clearcoat at grazing angles. It does not take off — it **dissolves to mist**. Interior is misty white void. Palette: `#0D0D0F` monolith basalt, `#F4F1EC` interior cream, `#B0B8BD` overcast sky.

**The biological UFO** (*Nope*'s Jean Jacket 2022) is kitbashed origami folds on bone-chain rigging: convex saucer at rest, radial-symmetry angel when unfurled, fabric-weave normal map, single square camera-eye primitive, green ribbon-lash tendrils. A 4–6 second elastic unfolding reveal along an authored fold hierarchy.

## Modern UAP archetypes, ten sensor-era primitives

Modern UAP design is subtractive. The catalog below uses canonical cases for exact dimensions and motion signatures.

**The Tic Tac** (USS Nimitz, November 14 2004) is the defining modern form. Navy Commander David Fravor, a Top Gun graduate leading the Black Aces squadron off San Diego, described **a smooth white elongated shape roughly 40 feet long, the size of his F/A-18F, with no wings, no exhaust, no rotors, no visible propulsion, no markings**, hovering 50 feet above a patch of churning white water as if something large were submerged beneath. It mirrored Fravor's spiral descent, then accelerated across his nose and vanished, reacquired on radar 60 miles away within a minute. Weapons Systems Officer Chad Underwood's ATFLIR footage (FLIR1) shows a small oblong heat source. **Generator**: capsule primitive (cylinder with hemispherical caps), aspect ratio ~3:1, length 12 m, seamless ceramic-white BRDF, metallic 0.0, roughness 0.35, albedo `#EDEDE9`, no normal detail, clearcoat 0.2. Motion: mirror-maneuver controller that inverts observer velocity, plus instant-translation teleport with 1-frame motion blur streak. Below it: a **churning whitewater patch** with upward-normal particle emitter and no perimeter rippling (selective disturbance).

**The black triangle** (Belgian UFO Wave November 29 1989 – April 1990, Hudson Valley 1982–86, Phoenix Lights March 13 1997) is a flat equilateral plate. Belgian witnesses consistently described **dark triangular craft 30–200 meters (frequently cited as ~100 feet wide), silent, with three bright white lights at the corners and a red pulsating light in the center**. The Eupen gendarmes (Nov 29 1989) reported three powerful downward beams illuminating the field beneath, emanating from large circular surfaces near the corners. On March 30–31 1990, ground radar at Glons and Semmerzake tracked unknowns; two F-16s from Beauvechain achieved nine intercept attempts and transient locks on objects accelerating from 150 to over 1,100 knots and dropping from 9,000 to 5,000 feet in 20 seconds. The Petit-Rechain photograph (April 4 1990), **long the iconic image and confessed a hoax by photographer Patrick Maréchal on RTL in July 2011** (painted polystyrene with embedded flashlights), remains visually definitive for the archetype regardless. The Phoenix Lights of March 13 1997 — seen from Henderson, Nevada to Tucson — showed a **V-formation of five to ten amber lights appearing mile-wide**, silent, occluding stars behind (suggesting a solid body roughly a carpenter's-square 60° wedge), passing 100–150 feet above a Phoenix family. **Generator**: equilateral plate with 3 corner omni-lights + 1 center red, concave underside with single center dome, hull `#0A0A0C`, running-light amber `#FFA62B`, center red `#C1272D`. Motion: rigid-body slide about center-of-mass with zero roll, silent hover bob (amplitude 0.2–0.8 m, period 2.5–4 s), V-formation boids with strict slot constraints and leader great-circle path.

**The orb/sphere** (USS Omaha July 15 2019, GOFAST January 2015, Chilean Navy November 11 2014) is a single sphere, radius 0.5–3 m. The USS Omaha Combat Information Center recorded **a six-foot-wide spherical, illuminated, solid-mass object** in black-hot IR descending into the Pacific off San Diego at 32°29'N 119°22'W, 11 PM PST, with minimum 14 targets in the sequence moving at 40–138 knots, "unable to discern origin, nor launch or landing points." Three-sensor radar correlation. No wreckage recovered — the class is officially characterized as **transmedium**. The Chilean Navy Airbus Cougar AS-532, flying 4,500 feet north of Santiago with a WESCAM MX-15 FLIR on November 11 2014, filmed a "flat, elongated structure" for 9 minutes 12 seconds that ejected a hot plume twice; CEFAA could not identify it (though later Metabunk analysis matched Iberia flight IB6830's A340 contrail). **Generator**: single sphere, matte-metallic or translucent white, fresnel glow `F = F0 + (1 - F0) * pow(1 - dot(N,V), p)` with p in 3–5. For IR rendering, single-channel luminance → LUT remap (black-hot: hot→black; white-hot: hot→white) with radiometric saturation halo. Varying speed 46–158 mph. Water entry with no splash or shockwave.

**The diamond** (Calvine, Scotland August 4 1990; Rendlesham Forest December 26–28 1980) is a rhombic prism. Two hotel workers above Calvine photographed **a ~30 meter dark-grey almost-black metallic-sheen diamond-shaped object hanging silent in the sky for ~10 minutes** alongside a low-passing Harrier, then ascending vertically at high speed. Six color photos were taken; the MoD analyst concluded "not fakes" and the only surviving print, held by RAF press officer Craig Lindsay since 1990, was released via Sheffield Hallam University in June 2022. Nick Pope, who ran the MoD UFO desk 1991–1994, had an enlarged copy on his office wall. Rendlesham Forest ("Britain's Roswell") added ground-trace and triangular-impression evidence, radiation readings, and Sgt. Jim Penniston's account of a metallic triangular craft with glowing lights and hieroglyphic-like symbols near RAF Woodbridge on December 26 1980. **Generator**: rhombic prism, 1:1.2 aspect, surface `#1B1B1F` near-black metallic sheen (metallic 0.9, roughness 0.15), no visible lights or portholes, zero audio signature, vertical-only exit acceleration.

**The teardrop/jellyfish** (Aguadilla, Puerto Rico April 25 2013 DHS FLIR; Al Taqaddum airbase, Iraq October 2018 "jellyfish UAP" released by Jeremy Corbell January 2024) is **a rounded circular body 3–5 feet across with dangling appendages**. Aguadilla's US Customs and Border Protection DC-8 WESCAM MX-15D thermal video tracked a glowing object for 3 minutes 54 seconds moving 40–120 mph across Rafael Hernández airport runway, into the Atlantic, submerging, re-emerging, **splitting into two**, with radar-corroborated speeds touching 1,200 mph. The Iraq footage, filmed from an aerostat, shows a roughly oval body with **three dangling tentacle-like appendages changing thermal polarity black↔grey↔white**, gliding over the base, descending into a lake for 17 minutes, then departing at high speed. Former Marine Michael Cincoski on the base nicknamed it "the spaghetti monster." **Generator**: spheroidal core + three flexible ribbon/tendril appendages with curl-noise animated normals, thermal polarity inversion over 2–3 seconds (emissive channel flips hot/cold sign), transmedium water-entry with zero splash.

**The boomerang/chevron** is the Phoenix Lights / Hudson Valley / Arnold 1947 crescent. Five to ten lights at 60° wedge apices, lights steady amber, V-formation at 0.8–1.2× craft span lateral spacing, drift velocity 20–40 m/s, star-occlusion silhouette alpha mask (craft reads as "hole punched in star-field"). Palette: amber `#FFA62B` on silhouette black `#0A0A0C`.

**The cube/box**, rarer but now canonized — *Navy TR-3B rumors, cube-inside-sphere multi-sensor reports described by Ryan Graves and cited in Unidentified flying object summaries*: a sphere encasing a cube was reportedly observed between two F-18s flying 100 feet apart. ISS livestream "cubes" are mostly debris (antenna covers, thermal blankets) but seed the visual. **Generator**: axis-aligned cube radius 1–3 m, chrome or matte-black, optional translucent sphere surrounding it, hover with zero rotation (perfect axial lock).

**The modern cigar/cylinder** — described in multiple 2021-era Navy reports, distinct from the smaller Tic Tac. Elongated 60–100+ feet, matte-white or polished, no wings, no contrail, often silent. Capsule primitive, aspect 8:1 to 12:1.

**Gimbal** (USS Theodore Roosevelt off Florida, January 21 2015) is its own archetype: a **dark saucer/top-shaped silhouette in black-hot IR with a white cold-halo around it**, radar-corroborated to be within 10 nautical miles with 4–6 accompanying unknowns, stopping and reversing direction with no radius of turn, then rotating ~90° from horizontal to vertical while maintaining flight path against 120-knot winds. Mick West's competing hypothesis treats it as rotating jet-engine glare with derotating background clouds revealing a camera gimbal-roll artifact. Either way the **visual signature is canonical**: saucer silhouette, white halo, smooth 90° rotation, black-hot IR, crosshair reticle, telemetry strings, "Gimb" status text. **Generator**: oblate disc with inverted radiometric halo, metallic 0.95, emissive cold-glow ring offset 1.3× radius, smooth axial rotation easing over 0.6 s.

**GO FAST** (same carrier, 2015) is a **small white smooth oval**, no wings, no tail, no exhaust plume, no visible propulsion, apparent high-speed low-altitude skim over ocean (actually a parallax illusion per West). Generator: sub-meter white ovoid, near-surface altitude, parallax-heavy camera-pursuit framing.

**The plasma portal/doorway** is the emerging speculative class — spectral-blue Cherenkov glow `#2BA6FF`, ultraviolet edge `#6A00FF`, ionization fringe `#B8F1FF`, void black `#05060A`. Raymarched cone frustum or torus with 3D Perlin on isosurface, branching L-system lightning.

## Visual and shader language principles

**The 50s-to-UAP axis is additive-to-subtractive.** Fifties grammar adds rivets, fins, saw-toothed rim lights, spinning Bakelite details, solid primary-color death-rays, and lens-flare "glints" as photographic authenticity. Modern UAP grammar subtracts until only a featureless primitive remains, framed by sensor chrome: crosshair reticle, mode text (NAR / FLIR / BLK HOT / WHT HOT), telemetry strings at frame edges, scan-line judder, autotrack lock-box that jumps on parallax-induced "instant acceleration," and zoom-step discrete rescales.

**FLIR/IR vocabulary** is now its own visual language. Render grainy 640×480-or-lower monochrome with hot-white or hot-black polarity. **Thermal bloom halo saturates where point sources overwhelm detector pixels** (radiometric saturation). Add per-column fixed-pattern noise and 1/f temporal noise. Glare/rotation halos around engines can appear as rotating craft (the GIMBAL artifact) — a useful ambiguity generator. Quantize to 8-bit grayscale, motion jitter at 24–30 fps with occasional single-frame rescales to sell authenticity.

**No-propulsion cues** are the cheapest way to read "UAP" in any frame: no exhaust plume, no heat signature on IR where expected, no condensation trail, no ionization glow, no rotor downwash, no audible engine, **water surface unrippled despite hovering**, leaves unmoved on foreground trees, dust not kicked up. IR shows the object cooler than or equal to ambient rather than engine-hot. Pair with tilt-to-maneuver (rigid-body pivot about center-of-mass with no arc or roll coupling — physically wrong for aircraft, visually correct for UFO) and instant translation (position teleport with a 1-frame motion blur streak and brief refraction shock, optionally leaving a residual after-image that fades over 6 frames).

**Light behavior taxonomy.** Seven canonical behaviors generate most of the corpus: steady constellation (Phoenix V, Belgian triangle); strobe-sequence (1950s rims, CE3K scouts); spectral-light conversation (CE3K mothership); corona/bloom (Gulf Breeze, orb reports); cloaking fade (star-occlusion alpha mask); beam-drop (abduction canon); and instant translation (Tic Tac, sophons). Day vs. night readings differ fundamentally — night is light-dominated (bloom, flare, long-exposure smears, reduced surface detail), day is silhouette-dominated (hard-edged dark against bright sky, rim-lighting on upper curve, heat shimmer, occasional iridescent mercury sheen, dusk "magic hour" for witness-photo convention).

## Generative parameters, a parametric toolkit

**Primitives by archetype:** torus + oblate lens + capsule dome (classic saucer); radial disc with fractal greeble + punched point-light layer (mothership); flat equilateral plate + corner lights (triangle); capsule 3:1 (Tic Tac); sphere 0.5–3 m (orb); rhombic prism 1:1.2 (diamond); spheroid core + three ribbon tendrils (jellyfish); cube with optional encasing sphere; capsule 8–12:1 (modern cigar); ovoid/lozenge 1:2.8 (Arrival shell); origami fold rig with square eye (Nope); prolate spheroid at ≥0.999 reflectivity (3BP droplet).

**Shader effects:** fresnel glow with p∈[3,5] and era-palette emissive rim; 6-tap Gaussian bloom at ½ and ¼ res with anamorphic 8:1 horizontal streak at night; raymarched volumetric beams (48 steps, density 0.25–0.7, 3D curl noise octave 3, Stokes-drag dust particles for abduction variant); IR thermal bloom with luminance remap to sensor LUT, radiometric saturation halo scaled by log(T/T_amb); plasma discharge via animated 3D Perlin isosurface with L-system branching lightning; mercury surface at metallic 1.0, roughness 0.02–0.08, anisotropic tangent along longitudinal axis, UV-offset liquid-flow normal map at amplitude 0.003; ceramic-white UAP at metallic 0.0, roughness 0.35, subsurface 0.4mm, albedo `#EDEDE9`, clearcoat 0.2; cloaking fade via 3D curl-noise dissolve threshold over 0.8s with refractive distortion IOR 1.02–1.08; black-monolith basalt via triplanar basalt photogrammetry at roughness 0.6 with grazing-angle iridescent clearcoat.

**Animation vocabulary:** hover bob (vertical sinusoid, amplitude 0.2–0.8 m, period 2.5–4 s, 10% phase jitter, zero pitch/roll coupling); tilt-to-bank (rigid ±45° lateral rotation, ease-in-out 0.6s, no inertial drift); instant translation; falling-leaf descent (alternating ±30° tilt, vertical velocity 1–3 m/s, lateral sway period 1.5 s — the Zamora/Trindade/Gulf Breeze profile); V-formation drift (boids with strict slot constraints, leader great-circle path, zero roll); cloaking fade (opacity 0.9→0 over 2–3 s with intensified edge fresnel and outward shimmer ripple); mirror-maneuver (inverts observer velocity vector, asymptotically matches altitude — Tic Tac signature); spectral-light conversation (per-segment emissive driven by audio waveform, 120 ms crossfades); unfolding reveal (origami fold hierarchy with elastic easing over 4–6 s).

**Environmental interactions** separate UAP from aircraft. Zero downwash on foliage, dust, water within a 2× craft-radius bubble. EM interference: CRT-scanline glitch on any in-scene screen, compass-needle spin, streetlight flicker at 60 Hz with random dropouts. Cloud interaction: disc-shaped hole in volumetric clouds (Nope, O'Hare) or lenticular cap-cloud attached to craft (Trent, CE3K), displacement radius 1.5× craft radius. Post-landing ground scorch with ringed three-tier desiccation decal. Water disturbance: selective whitewater patch directly beneath hovering craft with zero perimeter rippling (the Nimitz signature). Cherenkov/ionization rim: thin spectral-blue ring on leading edge at implied hypersonic velocities, faint at subsonic hover. Night-sky occlusion: star-field alpha multiplied by soft-edged silhouette mask so the craft reads as a hole punched in the background.

## How the archetypes read together

The striking through-line is that **the UAP canon has inverted its own grammar twice** — once around 1977 when Trumbull replaced hulls with light, and again around 2017 when the 2017 NYT leak replaced light with absence-framed-by-sensor. The pulp saucer was hardware. The CE3K mothership was luminescence. The Tic Tac is missing data. For a generative system, this means **the strongest "UFO reads" come not from adding UFO-ness to a primitive but from subtracting physics from it** — no wings, no exhaust, no shadow, no downwash, no sonic signature, no inertial coupling. Everything else is styling.

The second insight is that **canonical cases cluster around specific sensor modalities**, and sensor modality is itself a design choice. Classic cases live in dusk Kodachrome and overhead power-lines. Belgian-wave and Hudson-Valley cases live in underlit amateur 35mm. The modern canon lives in ATFLIR, WESCAM, and aerostat IR. The *same primitive* at the same scale reads as kitsch in one and as authentic in another — because the frame around it, not the object, carries the era.

Third, the biological turn (*Nope*'s Jean Jacket, Iraq's jellyfish) and the informational turn (*3 Body Problem*'s sophons and droplet) are **the emerging axes for novel design**. Both reject vehicle metaphors in favor of organisms or pure information-surfaces — a natural direction for a generative system with access to origami rigging, curl-noise tendrils, mirror BRDFs, and retinal HUD overlays.

A good generative UFO system, then, exposes four master axes: *era* (which gates primitive, palette, motion, and grain), *sensor framing* (naked-eye dusk / 35mm photo / VHS / ATFLIR / aerostat), *physics-subtraction depth* (how many conventional signatures are removed), and *class* (vehicle / biological / informational). The twelve primitives and the era palettes above are enough to populate a rich corpus. The canonical cases supply the calibration.
